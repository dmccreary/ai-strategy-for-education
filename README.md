# AI Strategy for Education

[![License: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-CC%20BY--NC--SA%204.0-lightgrey.svg)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/)

![AI Strategy for Education cover](docs/img/cover.png)

This is an open intelligent textbook written for educational leaders, administrators,
and instructional designers who need a rigorous, practical guide to deploying AI in
their institutions. Unlike vendor-driven overviews, this book grounds every recommendation
in measurable outcomes, pedagogical research, and real-world governance frameworks.
It covers the full arc from foundational AI literacy and capability measurement through
project selection, learning telemetry, responsible AI, and long-range strategic planning —
giving readers both the vocabulary and the decision-making tools to lead confidently in
a rapidly changing landscape.

## What's Inside

The book is organized into 13 chapters that progress from foundational concepts to
advanced strategic planning. Early chapters build shared vocabulary and measurement
frameworks; middle chapters address practical execution — selecting projects, tracking
learning outcomes, and personalizing instruction; later chapters tackle governance,
ethics, and workforce transformation. The final chapter integrates everything into a
capstone strategic planning exercise.

1. AI Foundations — What Every Educator Needs to Know
2. Measuring the AI Capability Curve
3. Building Your AI Strategy
4. Generative AI, Intelligent Textbooks, and the Content Revolution
5. The Idea Funnel — Gathering, Registering, and Evaluating Ideas
6. Selecting Projects, Assigning Resources, and Evaluating Outcomes
7. Learning Telemetry, xAPI, and AI-Driven Personalization
8. New Pedagogical Models — The Alpha School and Beyond
9. Responsible AI — Ethics, Bias, Privacy, and Fairness
10. Academic Integrity, Equity, and Managing AI Risk
11. AI Governance, Policy, and Change Management
12. The Agentic AI Workforce in Education
13. Strategic Planning — SWOT, Roadmaps, and the Capstone Strategy

## Key Features

- **Interactive MicroSims** — 7 browser-based simulations let readers explore concepts
  such as AI capability curves, idea funnels, and learning telemetry without leaving
  the textbook.
- **Learning graph of 221 concepts** — a structured knowledge graph with dependency
  links enables adaptive navigation and prerequisite tracking.
- **214-term glossary** — precise, non-circular definitions for every major term used
  in the book.
- **63 FAQ questions** — anticipates the questions educators most commonly ask, with
  concise, authoritative answers.
- **195 quiz questions** — 15 multiple-choice questions per chapter support formative
  assessment and self-study.
- **30 diagrams and visual elements** — original figures illustrate frameworks such as
  the METR capability curve, the idea funnel, and the AI governance lifecycle.
- **Chapter reference lists** — curated citations and further-reading links for all 13
  chapters.
- **Built with MkDocs Material** — clean, responsive, mobile-friendly design with
  full-text search.

## Running Locally

```bash
git clone https://github.com/dmccreary/ai-strategy-for-education.git
cd ai-strategy-for-education
pip install mkdocs-material
mkdocs serve
```

Visit [http://127.0.0.1:8000/ai-strategy-for-education/](http://127.0.0.1:8000/ai-strategy-for-education/)

## Author

Dan McCreary is a technology strategist, author, and educator with deep expertise in
graph databases, AI, and knowledge representation. He has spent the last decade helping
organizations build knowledge graphs and deploy AI at scale, and more recently has
focused on applying those tools to education — building intelligent textbooks, learning
graphs, and AI strategy frameworks for schools and universities. He is the author of
several books on graph technology and a frequent speaker at data and AI conferences.
